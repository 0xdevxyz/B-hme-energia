# GEO / LLMO Research – AI Search Optimization

## What is GEO/LLMO?
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) / LLM Optimization (LLMO) = making your content visible and citable in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.

## Key Signals (2025/2026)

### 1. llms.txt (emerging standard)
- Plain text file at domain root
- Summarizes who you are and what you offer
- LLM crawlers read this to understand site content
- Analogous to robots.txt but for AI

### 2. Schema.org JSON-LD
- LocalBusiness schema → Google Gemini explicitly uses this
- FAQPage schema → direct source for Q&A answers in ChatGPT/Perplexity
- AggregateRating → signals trust and authority

### 3. FAQ Sections
- Q&A format matches how LLMs retrieve and present information
- Questions should mirror actual search queries
- Answers should be direct, factual, 2-4 sentences

### 4. E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
- Author attribution with name + title
- Third-person "about" text (Wikipedia style)
- Certifications listed explicitly
- Last-updated date visible

### 5. robots.txt AI-Crawler Allowlist
Allow these explicitly:
- GPTBot (ChatGPT)
- ClaudeBot (Anthropic/Claude)
- Google-Extended (Gemini)
- PerplexityBot (Perplexity)
- cohere-ai (Cohere)
- Applebot-Extended (Apple)

### 6. NAP Consistency (Name, Address, Phone)
- Must be identical everywhere: website, Google Business, directories
- LLMs cross-reference multiple sources
- Inconsistency → lower confidence → less likely to be cited

### 7. Text Structure
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- H2/H3 hierarchy that LLMs use as navigation
- Bullet lists over dense text blocks
- Direct answer pattern: "X is Y in Z"

## Priority Order (2026)
1. Google AI Overviews (2B monthly users) – needs Schema.org + E-E-A-T
2. ChatGPT (77% of AI referral traffic) – needs GPTBot allowance + structured content
3. Perplexity (15% of AI referral traffic) – needs PerplexityBot + citations
4. Claude / Gemini – ClaudeBot + Google-Extended allowances
